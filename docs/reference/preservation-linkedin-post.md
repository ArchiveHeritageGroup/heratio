## Post text

We spent a decade making archives findable, and this year making them intelligent. We forgot to ask whether any of it will still exist in fifty years.

That question is preservation - the unglamorous half of the archive. No demo, no headline, no applause. And AI just made it urgent, not optional:

- AI multiplies what you must keep. Every transcription, confidence score and audit log is a record in its own right.
- AI raises the authenticity bar. In a world of confident machine-generated text, fixity, provenance and immutable audit stop being academic and become load-bearing.
- The models themselves are ephemeral. "We used the 2026 model" is not an answer unless you preserved which model, which version, on which input.

Backup is not preservation. Sync is not preservation. "It's in the cloud" is not a strategy. The piece everyone skips is the one that matters most: one copy offline, air-gapped and immutable, checked against a known-good baseline.

Access is a promise about today. Preservation is a promise about the future. Only one of them is hard to keep.
