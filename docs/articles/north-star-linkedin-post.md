Every product needs one honest sentence that every feature has to earn its place against. Ours is:

**A culture you can talk to.**

Not a search box you have to already understand. A collection that answers a plain question, in the language you asked it in, grounded in the institution's own records, and honest about what it does not know.

That last part is the whole job. Anyone can put a chatbot on an archive. The hard, and only worthwhile, part is making it true:

- It answers from the institution's own corpus, and it shows its sources.
- When the records do not cover your question, it says "I don't have enough to answer that" instead of inventing a confident, wrong reply.
- It answers in your language through a real translation route, with extra care for the languages the big models serve worst, not less.
- And it does not stay flat: you can walk through a gallery as a 3D digital twin, ask a single object about itself, and even borrow an object from a partner institution, attributed and linked back to its owner.

For a memory institution, trust about the past is the entire asset. A fluent fabrication is not a shortcut. It is a self-inflicted wound. So we built the slow, honest version.

Built for the global GLAM sector. Jurisdiction-neutral at the core, with country-specific compliance as pluggable modules. Every AI call governed, metered and traceable.
