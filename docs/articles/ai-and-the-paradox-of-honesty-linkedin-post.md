# AI and the Paradox of Honesty - linked post (LinkedIn)

We tell ourselves AI is "honest" because it only reflects its data.

It isn't. A language model cannot lie, because lying means knowing the truth and choosing to depart from it. But for the same reason it cannot tell the truth either. It produces the most plausible-sounding answer, and when that answer is false it says so with exactly the same confidence as when it's right.

At the Rhodes / Post-Truth conference I called it a child genius: unmatched recall, no judgement. That is the machine we are now wiring into our archives.

For a memory institution this is the whole problem. An unverified AI output is not a record. It's a claim with no author and no chain of custody, dressed in the language of authority.

So the goal isn't "honest AI." It's accountable AI, and provenance is the one thing archives have always known how to do. In Heratio:

- Every AI call written to a tamper-evident, signed ledger, traceable to the exact model and input.
- Human review recorded on the record, attributed and countersigned.
- Content credentials (C2PA) on media, so an original can be told from a machine-altered copy.

None of this makes the model truthful. It makes it answerable.

The machine can't tell us what's true. The archive can guarantee every claim is traceable and answered for. In an age of fluent falsehood, that may be the essential job.
