## Post 1 - Launch (framework + workbook)

Most organisations are sitting on decades of records they cannot find: shared drives,
email, scanned boxes. The blocker for using AI on them was never the model. It was doing
it without losing control of governance, privacy, and the evidence trail.

So we published the framework we wished existed.

Vendor-neutral. Jurisdiction-neutral. Built for AI specialists and the records, legal, and
executive readers who have to sign it off. It comes with working tick-sheets: a minimum
viable deployment checklist, a vendor and acceptance-test pack, and a compliance control
catalog that maps regulation to concrete controls.

## Post 3 - Provenance and trust

"Can I trust what the AI told me about this record?"

That is the only question that matters in archival AI. The answer is provenance: what was
produced, who or what ran it, when, which model, and how confident.

If you cannot trace it, you cannot trust it. Here is what proper authority resolution and
provenance look like in practice.

https://heratio.theahg.co.za/articles

## Post 4 - Compliance (access-to-information + AI regulation)

AI in a gallery, library, archive, or museum is not exempt from the law.

Access-to-information deadlines, data-protection obligations, and new AI regulation all
land on the same records. The good news: the controls are the same even when the regimes
differ. Map the obligation once, configure the control once.

We break down what the rules mean for the heritage sector.
