# v1.154.399 (2026-07-22)

perf(term): skip prev/next navigation on very large taxonomies. Third and final pass on the term-page cost (v1.154.397 stopped the crash, v1.154.398 removed ~6s). Profiling the residual found nextTerm at ~3,500ms, and the root cause is structural rather than a bad plan: term_i18n.name is varchar(1024) but idx_term_i18n_culture_name_id indexes only a 191-char prefix (sub_part=191), and MySQL will not use a prefix index to satisfy an ORDER BY. So every ORDER BY term_i18n.name is a filesort no matter what - FORCE INDEX and STRAIGHT_JOIN both leave it at ~3.5s on taxonomy 35 (362,095 terms), and even a bare term_i18n LIMIT 1 with no joins costs 1.5s. Widening the index is not available: base AtoM table, and utf8mb4 caps a key at 3072 bytes / 768 chars against a 1024-char column. Adds NAV_MAX (20000) so prev/next is simply not issued above that size - it is a nicety, and its meaning is thin across a third of a million alphabetised terms. The listTerms sort (~3,800ms) is the same filesort and is the one remaining cost; fixing it needs the List tab to load lazily rather than being rendered eagerly into every term page, which is left as a follow-up
