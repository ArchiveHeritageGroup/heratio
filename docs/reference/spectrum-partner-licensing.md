# Spectrum Partner licensing - how to make Heratio "Spectrum compliant"

**Summary:** There is no "Spectrum certified user/system" exam or badge. Spectrum (the Collections Trust collections-management standard, UK) is free for non-commercial use. To have a commercial product such as Heratio recognised as *Spectrum compliant* and listed in the Collections Trust software directory, the vendor takes out a **commercial licence** and becomes a **Spectrum Partner**. This is a negotiated licence and fee arranged by direct contact, not an online registration form.

## The two levels

1. **Using Spectrum (free, non-commercial).** Anyone can download the standard and use it after agreeing to the licence conditions. A museum can run its documentation on Spectrum with no registration and no cost.
2. **Being recognised as a Spectrum-compliant product (commercial).** This is the Spectrum Partner route. Partners (Axiell, Vernon Systems, Zetcom, CollectionSpace, Collector Systems, MODES, Gallery Systems, etc.) license the standard commercially; their products can then be marketed as "Spectrum compliant" and appear in the Collections Trust software comparison directory.

## How to become a Spectrum Partner

- **Contact:** David Gelsthorpe, Chief Executive of Collections Trust - `davidg@collectionstrust.org.uk`. He is the named Partnership contact on the Spectrum Partners page. A general enquiries inbox also exists via the Collections Trust contact page.
- **Process:** direct conversation, not a web form. Ask about the Partnership steps, the commercial licence terms and fee (unpublished - negotiated), what is required for the product to be described as "Spectrum compliant", and how to be listed in the software directory.
- **Fee:** not published. Licensing income "helps maintain and develop the standard"; the figure is provided on enquiry.
- **International scope:** the current Partner list is weighted towards the UK, EU and US. Because Heratio targets Southern Africa, the wider SADC region, Europe and Asia-Pacific, confirm with Collections Trust that the Partner licence and directory listing apply globally.

## Why this matters for Heratio (compliance gap)

Heratio ships the `ahg-spectrum` package, which implements the Spectrum 5.1 procedures as part of a commercial platform. The Spectrum licensing terms state:

> "Under no circumstances may you develop products or services which may be deemed commercially to compete with the products or services of the Collections Trust, its partners or affiliates... translations, localisations or derivative works... shall in all cases be subject to express prior written consent from the Collections Trust."

So Heratio is currently making commercial use of the standard under only the non-commercial licence. Taking out the Spectrum Partner commercial licence closes a genuine compliance gap - it is not merely a marketing badge - and should be done before Heratio is marketed as Spectrum-compliant.
