# Heratio Virtual Exhibitions & Digital Twin
### Capability overview

Heratio turns a collection into an immersive, walkable virtual exhibition - a **digital twin** of a
gallery that anyone can explore from a browser, on a phone, or in VR. Curators build and manage the
space with drag-and-drop tools; visitors, students and researchers walk through it, ask an AI guide
questions, attend live openings, and experience objects in interactive 3D - all with no software to
install.

This document summarises what the platform can do, organised by the two audiences:

- **Visitors** - the public, students and researchers, with no account required.
- **Curators & staff** - authenticated users who create, curate and manage exhibitions.

---

## For visitors (no account required)

### Explore and walk through
- **Immersive 3D walkthrough.** A first-person tour of the gallery on desktop and mobile - move
  around, look at objects on pedestals, in glass display cases, or hung on the walls.
- **Interactive 3D objects.** Examine high-fidelity 3D models and photoreal Gaussian-splat captures
  of real artefacts, with smooth zoom, rotate and inspection.
- **Mobile companion.** An on-site phone experience (opened via a QR code in the physical gallery)
  with object cards and the AI guide.
- **Virtual reality.** Enter the exhibition in VR on a headset-capable browser.

### A knowledgeable AI guide
- **Ask the docent.** Conversational, multi-turn questions about any object or the exhibition as a
  whole, answered from the catalogue with no invented facts.
- **It leads the way.** The guide understands where you are standing, resolves "tell me about this",
  and offers to walk you to the next object worth seeing.
- **Spoken narration.** Natural voice narration for a hands-free, gallery-style experience.
- **Personal recommendations.** "You might also like" suggestions as you move through the show.

### Shared, social and live
- **See other visitors.** Real-time presence - explore alongside other people moving through the
  same space.
- **Guided live tours.** Follow a curator leading a live walkthrough.
- **Live openings.** Attend scheduled, ticketed opening events: reserve a free place, receive a
  ticket, and join the live walkthrough at event time as a named, co-present guest.
- **Leave your mark.** Add a wall note/annotation in the space.

### Accessible and open
- **Accessible tour.** A keyboard- and screen-reader-friendly text-and-narration alternative to the
  3D experience.
- **Open standards.** Each exhibition is published in open, interoperable formats (IIIF, scene
  export, and Linked Data / JSON-LD) so it can be cited, embedded and reused.

## For curators & staff

Everything visitors can do, plus a full authoring, management and analytics toolkit.

### Build the space (drag-and-drop, no coding)
- **Room builder.** Place objects on the floor, stand them on plinths, enclose them in glass display
  cases, or hang them on walls - then drag to arrange. Control each object's real-world size,
  orientation, lighting/spotlight and layering.
- **Furnish and finish.** Add benches, pedestals, cases, planters and rope barriers; set wall
  colours and images, floor and ceiling finishes, and upload your own decor.
- **Multi-room buildings.** Lay out whole buildings on a blueprint: multiple rooms that snap
  together, custom room shapes, doors, windows, corridors and multi-floor staircases.
- **Photoreal capture.** Back a room with a real-world 3D scan of your actual gallery.
- **Guided routes.** Define the order in which objects are visited for a curated journey.

### AI-assisted curation
- **Generative exhibitions.** Enter a theme and the system proposes a curated draft - selecting and
  arranging relevant objects with labels - which you then refine.
- **Authored audio tours.** Record a guided route with your own narration script and audio.
- **Live docent.** Lead a real-time guided tour that visitors follow through the space.

### A living digital twin (conservation & operations)
- **Live environmental monitoring.** Bind real light, temperature and humidity sensors to the space.
- **Conservation alerts.** Automatic warnings when readings fall outside preservation thresholds.
- **Forecasting.** Simulate and predict environmental conditions against the physical room.

### Understand your audience
- **Analytics dashboard.** Visitor and environmental trends over time, by room.
- **Visitor heatmaps.** A top-down view of where visitors spend their time.
- **Per-object attention.** See which objects truly hold attention versus those that are glanced at
  and skipped - so you can re-light, re-position or re-interpret with evidence.

### Events and publishing
- **Ticketed live openings.** Schedule openings with a host, capacity and start time; manage
  registrations and run the event live in the twin.
- **Knowledge-graph publishing.** Publish exhibitions into the institutional knowledge graph for
  cross-collection discovery and linked-data reuse.

## Why it matters for a university

- **Teaching & public engagement.** Turn collections into immersive, always-open exhibitions for
  students, researchers and the wider public - no specialist software or gallery visit required.
- **Evidence-based curation.** Audience analytics and per-object attention bring measurable insight
  to exhibition design.
- **Preservation-aware.** The digital twin links the virtual exhibition to live environmental
  monitoring and conservation thresholds for the real objects.
- **Inclusive by design.** Accessible alternatives and multi-device support (desktop, mobile, VR)
  widen reach.
- **Open and interoperable.** Standards-based publishing (IIIF, Linked Data) ensures the work is
  citable, embeddable and reusable across the sector.
