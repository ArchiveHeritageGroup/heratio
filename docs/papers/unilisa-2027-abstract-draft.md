Title:
  Repositioning the Archive for Industry 5.0: A Human-in-the-Loop AI
  Platform Integrating Records Management, Preservation, and Records-in-Contexts

Abstract (~290 words):

The transition from Industry 4.0's automation to Industry 5.0's
human-centric collaboration with AI demands a corresponding repositioning
of archival and information science. Records are no longer passive
deposits to be digitised; they are living assets requiring continuous
curation, ethical AI-assisted interpretation, and long-term preservation
that survives the technological lifecycles of the systems that created
them. Yet most heritage platforms still treat capture, description,
records management, and preservation as separate silos, and most
AI-augmented workflows treat the human archivist as a mere reviewer of
black-box output rather than a first-class collaborator.

This paper presents Heratio, an open-source archival platform that
integrates the full records lifecycle - from active document management
through ISAD(G) description, ISO 15489-conformant retention and disposal,
BagIt/OAIS preservation packaging, and RDF-graph-based discovery via the
Records-in-Contexts (RiC-CM/RiC-O) ontology. Each AI capability -
named-entity extraction, condition assessment from images, semantic
similarity search via sentence embeddings, classification rule inference
- operates under explicit human-in-the-loop confirmation and is
auditable end-to-end via PREMIS-shaped event logs. SHACL validation
against curated RiC shapes ensures that the speed of AI-assisted ingest
does not come at the cost of descriptive quality. Compliance frameworks
(ISO 15489, ISO 16175, MoReq2010, DoD 5015.2) are integrated as
automated assessments rather than annual paper exercises.

Drawing on production deployments serving multi-collection repositories
of African historical records, this paper demonstrates that
human-centric AI integration is not merely an ethical posture but an
operational necessity: records lose meaning when their context, retention
rules, and provenance chains are decoupled. By holding all four pillars
in one auditable system, Heratio offers a concrete model for
repositioning information science from digitisation as endpoint to
digital transformation as continuous practice.

Keywords: archival informatics, Records-in-Contexts, ISO 15489, OAIS,
human-in-the-loop AI, Industry 5.0, RiC-O, SHACL, BagIt, digital
preservation, African archives.
